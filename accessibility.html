Accessibility Statement

Last updated: May 12, 2026

Matchic is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people of all abilities. We are continuously improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards to make the app as inclusive as possible.

Our Commitment

We believe that wardrobe management should be accessible to everyone, regardless of ability. Our goal is to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 Level AA standards for our website and to follow platform-specific accessibility best practices for our mobile applications (iOS and Android).

Accessibility Features in the App

The Matchic mobile application has been designed with the following accessibility considerations:

Visual

  • Sufficient color contrast: All text and interactive elements meet or exceed WCAG AA contrast ratios
  • Scalable text: The app supports system font size settings; text can be enlarged without loss of content or functionality
  • Dark mode support: Full dark theme for reduced eye strain
  • Non-text content alternatives: Icons are paired with text labels where possible

Screen Reader Compatibility

  • iOS (VoiceOver): All interactive elements have meaningful accessibility labels; images include descriptive alt text generated by AI where applicable
  • Android (TalkBack): Proper content descriptions and navigation landmarks are implemented
  • Dynamic content updates are announced via live regions

Motor & Dexterity

  • All actions can be performed via taps; no complex gestures are required for critical functionality
  • Touch targets are designed to be at least 48x48dp as recommended by platform guidelines
  • AI-powered auto-tagging reduces the need for manual text input

Cognitive

  • Consistent navigation structure throughout the app
  • Clear, simple language in user-facing text
  • Onboarding coachmarks help users understand features step by step
  • Photos and visual cues supplement text instructions

Website (matchic.app)

Our landing page website is built with accessibility in mind:

  • Semantic HTML5 structure with proper landmark regions
  • Keyboard-navigable interactive elements
  • ARIA labels where needed
  • Responsive design that works across screen sizes and zoom levels
  • No auto-playing media, flashing content, or time limits

Known Limitations

We acknowledge the following areas where accessibility can be improved:

  • AI-generated content: Auto-tags and outfit recommendations generated by AI may not always be perfectly descriptive. We are working to improve the quality of AI-generated alt text and labels.
  • Camera-based features: The scan feature requires visual interaction. We are exploring alternative input methods for future releases.
  • Third-party components: Some open-source UI components may have limited accessibility support. We actively monitor and update these as fixes become available.

Testing & Compliance

We test our app using:

  • iOS VoiceOver and Android TalkBack screen readers
  • Platform accessibility scanners (Xcode Accessibility Inspector, Android Accessibility Scanner)
  • Manual testing with keyboard-only navigation
  • Color contrast analyzers

Feedback & Contact

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of Matchic. If you encounter any accessibility barriers or have suggestions for improvement, please contact us:

  • Email: support@matchic.app (subject: "Accessibility Feedback")
  • GitHub: Report accessibility issue
  • Response time: We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within 5 business days

Continuous Improvement

Accessibility is an ongoing effort. We review and update our practices regularly as part of our development cycle. This statement was last reviewed on May 12, 2026 and will be reviewed and updated at least annually or whenever significant changes are made to the app's interface.
